Flight Offsetter
What does the offset transaction do?

The carbon offset button initiates an on-chain transaction which exchanges the supplied tokens into NCT tokens on sushiswap. It then takes these NCT tokens to exchange them into the tokens of the carbon reduction project. The project tokens are then irrevocably retired using the infrastructure of the toucan bridge. All these steps are done in one transaction and can be verified on chain. Here is an example of such a transaction starting with USDC as an input token: offset transaction.

Which project is supported when offsetting the carbon credits?

The offsetter automatically retires token from a carbon reduction project provided by the toucan bridge. The offset project at the moment is a forest protection project called North Pikounda REDD+ in the Republic of the Congo.

Can I choose a carbon credit project to support?

Not yet. At the moment the offsetter automatically retires carbon certificates by the default project provided by the the toucan bridge. In future iterations the offsetter will make it possible to chose from the several dozens of projects in the NCT pool.

Can I fill in a custom number of carbon to offset?

Yes. Just click on the box to the left of the TCO2 units, above the green line and manually fill in the amount of carbon you want to offset.

Why is the calculated carbon emission different for each flight offset calculator?

To calculate the carbon emission, a lot of assumptions need to be made. Some influencing factors are the weather, the airplane type, the seat occupancy, cargo load, emission from the airport infrastructure, carbon emission from jet fuel production. Most of these factors cannot be known for a specific flight and therefore need to be calculated from general number of average emissions of the flight industry. As our approach is to make it as simple as possible for the user to offset their emission, we do not provide such a user configurable flight emission estimator, but focus on the typical emission for a certain flight distance. Google flights uses airplane information to estimate carbon emission for each route. These calculated carbon emission can be manually filled in the disCarbon flight offsetter and be offset on chain.

Who is disCarbon?

disCarbon are danceratopz and haurog. Our goal is to accelerate the adoption of on-chain carbon credits. The existing off-chain carbon market is opaque, illiquid and benefits middlemen as apposed to the underlying carbon offset projects that positively impact our planet. A liquid on-chain market allows carbon offset projects to manage their own carbon credits, enabling them to obtain market prices when required. Our hope is that this leads to the faster creation of high quality carbon offset projects.
